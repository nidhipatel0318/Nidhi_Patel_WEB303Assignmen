Biography

Milkha Singh, also known as "The Flying Sikh," was a legendary Indian sprinter known for his outstanding achievements in athletics.

Achievements

Milkha Singh represented India in various international competitions, and his most famous moment was the fourth-place finish in the 400m race at the 1960 Rome Olympics.

Legacy

Milkha Singh's legacy continues to inspire young athletes in India, and his life story was portrayed in the biographical sports drama film "Bhaag Milkha Bhaag."

Early Life

Milkha Singh was born on October 20, 1929, in Govindpura, Punjab, British India. He faced many challenges during his childhood, including the partition of India in 1947.

Career

During his career, Milkha Singh won several gold medals in the Asian Games and represented India in multiple international competitions. He was a national hero in the world of athletics.

Personal Life

Milkha Singh married Nirmal Kaur, a former Indian volleyball captain. They had three children, and their family life was an essential part of his personal journey.
